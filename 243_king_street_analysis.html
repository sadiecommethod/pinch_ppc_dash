Strategic Analysis

Key Insights & Observations

What the numbers reveal beyond the headline figures.

📈

ADR Premium Is the Game-Changer

The $180/night ADR gap ($545 vs $725) is the single largest driver of the EBITDA difference. At 1,460 occupied nights, this alone generates an additional ~$263,000 in revenue before expenses. This premium reflects direct booking power, better positioning, and dynamic pricing — not just demand.

🔄

The Commission Paradox

Method earns 42% more revenue yet pays $3,727 less in commissions. On the DIY path, OTAs (AirBnB, VRBO) extract significant fees per booking. Method's direct booking infrastructure recaptures that spread and keeps it with the owner — a structural advantage that compounds over time.

💰

Both Scenarios Are Strong — The Gap Is Massive

A 60% EBITDA margin on DIY is genuinely excellent by any hospitality benchmark. But Method at 63% margin on 42% more revenue is exceptional. The incremental $245K annually means Method pays for itself in full after roughly 30 days of operation.

🎯

Marketing Investment Justifies Itself Immediately

Method spends $29,650 more on Sales & Marketing, but generates $321,907 more in revenue — a 10.9x return on incremental marketing spend. The higher advertising line isn't a cost; it's the engine driving the ADR premium and reduced OTA dependency.

🏗️

Cost Ratios Improve Across the Board

Even though absolute dollars spent on payroll, POM, and utilities rise, nearly every cost category shrinks as a % of revenue under Method. Rooms payroll drops from 8% to 6%, total rooms expenses from 14% to 11%. Scale and professionalism create efficiency.

🚀

F&B Upside Not Yet Modeled

The footnote on this analysis explicitly flags that F&B introduction could enhance performance further via separate management. Neither scenario captures this revenue stream. A curated F&B offering at the ADR levels Scenario B commands would likely be well-received and accretive.

The Bottom Line for 243 King Street

Both paths generate solid returns on a 5-room property. But the gap between DIY and professionally managed is not incremental — it's transformational. Method doesn't just run the property better; it repositions the asset at a fundamentally higher price point, reduces OTA dependency, and delivers a profit outcome that a self-managed OTA approach simply cannot replicate without the same infrastructure, market access, and operational discipline.
