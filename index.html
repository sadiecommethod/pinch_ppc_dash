Strategic Assessment

The Pinch's current non-brand campaign has strong volume but a significant efficiency problem. The "Search – Non-Brand Best/Charleston" ad group is the engine (324K impressions, $122K revenue, 1.22x ROAS) yet 50% of IS is lost to budget, signaling budget constraint is actively limiting growth. Meanwhile, "Boutique (*)" ad group is essentially dead — 148 impressions, zero clicks, zero revenue — consuming structural overhead. The proposed overhaul focuses spend on high-intent, conversion-proven clusters and introduces Luxury/Experience segmentation to capture The Pinch's positioning premium.

Why 35% to Best Hotel

The "best hotel charleston sc" keyword alone generated $71K in tracked revenue. This group is the top revenue engine. Budget constraint is already costing IS — this allocation addresses that directly while keeping CPA sustainable at ~$1,275 target.

✨

Why 20% to Luxury

Luxury intent searchers have lower price sensitivity and higher ADR. Current luxury keywords show 1.34–1.66x ROAS with room to grow. Investing here defends The Pinch's premium positioning and aligns spend with the property's brand identity.

📍

Why 17.5% to Downtown

"Charleston sc hotels" broad generated 4,115 clicks and $57K revenue. A well-structured geo/location group with tight negative keyword management turns this into a reliable volume driver. Needs search term report scrubbing monthly.

🏨

Why 15% to Boutique

The Pinch IS a boutique hotel. Searchers using "boutique hotel" terms self-select as aligned prospects. This segment needs proper structure — the current Boutique (*) group is broken. Rebuild it cleanly with 3-4 tight keywords and strong RSA copy.

💑

Why 12.5% to Experience

Occasion/romantic searchers are highest ADR. They're booking for anniversaries, honeymoons, and special weekends — and willing to pay a premium. The 4th-night-free offer is a perfect hook. Start with $500/mo as a test; scale if CPA looks good in 60 days.

🎯

60-Day Review Trigger

Reallocate budget from any group running above $900 CPA or below 0.8x ROAS after 60 days of data. The Experience group should hit >$0 revenue within 45 days or budget should shift to Best Hotel scaling. Review IS Lost to Budget monthly — if Best Hotel >40%, increase its allocation first.

Current Campaign Audit
An honest assessment of what's working, what's broken, and what's being left on the table in the current non-brand structure.

🔴 Issues to Fix

Boutique (*) is dead. 148 impressions, -99.2% decline, zero clicks, zero revenue. The group structure or bidding is broken. Pausing immediately and rebuilding is recommended.
8 keywords with 0 impressions. These waste account structure overhead and dilute Quality Scores. Pause all immediately.
50% IS lost to budget on the top-performing "Best/Charleston" group. Leaving revenue on the table by underfunding the best performer.
Expanded Text Ads (ETAs) are paused but still in account structure (Hotel Best, Hotel Boutique, Hotel Luxury groups). Google deprecated ETAs — these should be fully removed and replaced with RSAs.
ROAS declined from 2.40x to 1.26x overall despite revenue growing 36%. Spend grew faster than revenue — efficiency is eroding.
CPA increased from $653 to $1,237 — an 89% increase. This is the clearest signal that the current structure needs optimization.

🟢 What's Working

"best hotel charleston sc" — the single best keyword. 6,333 clicks, $71K revenue, 1.79x ROAS, Quality Score 4 (room to improve copy relevance).
Revenue trajectory is strong — $197K non-brand revenue, +36% YoY. The underlying demand is real; the issue is efficiency not volume.
RSA copy strategy is correct — all active ad groups are already using Responsive Search Ads. Good foundation to build on.
Luxury keywords are performing — "luxury hotels charleston sc" generated $34.1K revenue at 1.66x ROAS. This segment deserves more budget than current allocation.
Landing page strategy is solid — URLs to thepinch.com/luxury-hotel, /downtown, /luxury-boutique-hotel show good landing page segmentation intent already in place.
4th-night-free offer is a powerful hook already present in brand copy — should be more prominently featured in non-brand RSA headlines.
